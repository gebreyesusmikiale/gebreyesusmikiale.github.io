Current Project

Borders of
the Mind

Every year, thousands of young Africans risk their lives crossing to Europe, driven by a dangerous myth — that Europe means wealth and Africa means poverty.

This photography book and travelling exhibition holds both continents up as mirrors. Images of forgotten poverty. Images of overlooked beauty in African communities. One message: the border that truly matters is not on any map.

About

A camera carried
across three countries

I am Gebreyesus Mehari Mikiale — a Tigrayan photographer currently based in Florence, Italy, where I am completing a Master's degree in Software Science and Technology at the University of Florence on a UN scholarship.

I started photography in 2018. When war came to my region in 2021, I fled — travelling overland from Addis Ababa through Moyale to Nairobi, one of the most dangerous migration routes in East Africa.

"In Nairobi I became a refugee. I also became a professional photographer. My camera was how I survived — and how I made sense of the world."

In 2025 I came to Italy. I kept shooting — Italian streets, Italian faces, the ordinary moments that locals no longer see. I volunteer as a photographer for an NGO and share my work on Instagram.
